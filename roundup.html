Weekend Roundup

29 March 2026

🏃 Parkrun

The headline story this week is one that's been 10 weeks in the making. 24 RTR members were out at parkrun across 7 venues on Saturday - and the biggest occasion of the morning was the graduation of our Couch to 5K group at Heaton Park.

Heaton Park ⭐

What a morning at Heaton Park. After 10 weeks of training, turning up, and encouraging each other, 8 runners crossed the finish line together, many completing their very first parkrun. Huge congratulations to Alicia Ricketts, Barbara Fortune, Christine Ince, Julie Denny, Rachel Fiske, Sairah Rehman, Tanja Brajkovic, and Tobias Wroe - every single one of you should be incredibly proud. And a heartfelt thank you to the run leaders who gave their time over 10 weeks to make it happen. This is exactly what RTR is all about 💜

There was a brilliant turnout of RTR regulars at Heaton Park to join the graduates, among 817 runners out on a busy morning. Neil Naisbitt headed up the RTR pack, with Dale Richards close behind. Cheryl Stewardson ran solidly, and Jacqui Warden had a lovely morning out on the course with her dog in tow 🐕 Great to see Ken Smith back at Heaton after a run of parkruns in Western Australia! Fiona Case and Helen Naisbitt both had fine mornings out, while Stephen Crowe and Chris Grayshon came through within a second of each other. Tracy Wroe was in great form alongside them, and Georgia Taylor had an excellent outing - claiming a course PB on her return to the park 👏 Derek Case rounded things off with a parkwalk at the back.

Alderford Lake

Ros Dines headed to Alderford Lake in Shropshire for a scenic lakeside outing, flying the RTR flag well among the 110-strong field.

Bolton

Josh Young put in a great run at Bolton, picking up a course PB in the process 👏 Paula Crosland also had an excellent morning out.

Clarence

A brilliant day for Michael Aiken, who reached his 50th parkrun at Clarence - a landmark worth celebrating! 🎉 And if that wasn't enough, he finished 3rd male in a field of 75 runners. A superb run on a very special parkrun morning 💜

Northwich

Andrew and Alison Whitehead headed to Northwich in Cheshire for a first visit to this riverside venue. A new one ticked off for two of RTR's most well-travelled parkrunners!

The Cinder Track, Whitby

Kath Biddle made the trip to Whitby for a first visit to the Cinder Track - a historic trail running along a former railway line with sweeping views over the Esk Valley and the iconic Whitby Viaduct. Very much worth the journey. Kath finished 3rd female out of 135 runners. Outstanding running! 🏃‍♀️

Ziegelwiese, Germany

Julie Smith showed she was in excellent shape ahead of the Berlin Half Marathon by fitting in a parkrun at Ziegelwiese in Germany on Saturday morning - a confident run on her first visit to the course. The signs were very good indeed for Sunday.

🏅 Races

Saturday 28 March

Generali Prague Half Marathon

The Generali Prague Half Marathon is one of the most beautiful road races in the world - a fast, flat course past Charles Bridge, Prague Castle, the National Theatre and the Rudolfinum in the heart of one of Europe's most spectacular cities. It's also one of the 6 races in the Superhalfs series, and Paul Cox took it on this Saturday, completing his 4th race in the series. Brilliant running in a brilliant city 💜

Sunday 29 March

Generali Berlin Half Marathon

Julie Smith followed up her German parkrun with a stunning performance at the Generali Berlin Half Marathon - a World Athletics Elite Label event and one of the most iconic road races on the planet, with 40,000 runners taking on a fast, flat course past the Reichstag, the Victory Column and the Brandenburg Gate. Berlin was also Julie's 4th Superhalfs race. A remarkable European weekend from start to finish 🏃‍♀️

Weekend Roundup

14 March 2026

🏃 Parkrun

What a morning! 12 RTR runners were out across 6 venues on Saturday — with milestone moments, top-3 finishes, and a trip all the way to Bath. Here's how everyone got on.

Barnsley

Kath Biddle made the trip across to Barnsley parkrun — a course that winds through the park and delivers a solid test on every visit. Kath had a brilliant morning, finishing 1st in her age category. A superb run from Kath! 💜

Bolton

Ros Dines was out at Bolton parkrun, putting in a great effort on a course she clearly knows well. Well done Ros — always brilliant to see you out there on a Saturday morning.

Brickfields Park

Andrew and Alison Whitehead headed all the way down to Bath for the weekend and kicked things off with a parkrun at Brickfields Park — a multi-terrain route that weaves from the park along the Linear Park path, gradually climbing toward the Devonshire Tunnel before looping back for a grassy finish. Andrew was first of the RTR pair home, with Alison running strongly alongside. And as it turns out, they weren't quite done with Bath just yet… 👀

Clarence

A standout Saturday morning at Clarence for Michael Aiken, who ran to a brilliant 2nd place overall — a superb performance in a field of 71 runners. Stephen Crowe also represented RTR well at Clarence, putting in a solid and composed run over the course.

Heaton Park

The biggest RTR turnout of the day, with 4 members at Heaton. Dale Richards was first back for the team, running with the confidence of someone who knows every corner of this course. Cheryl Stewardson was in great form too, putting in another impressive run — she's looking really consistent at the moment. It was also lovely to see Angela Pradena and Ann Roughley out enjoying the park and taking in the fresh air on a Saturday morning 💜

Worsley Woods

A very special morning at Worsley Woods for Derek Case, who completed his 200th parkrun! That's an incredible milestone — 200 Saturday mornings of lacing up and heading out, rain or shine. Huge congratulations to Derek from everyone at RTR 🎉 Fiona Case was the first RTR runner home at the venue, running with her usual quiet determination over one of the group's favourite local courses.

🏅 Races

Saturday 14 March

Chicken Run Fell Race

5 RTR members headed to Hayfield in the Peak District to take on the Chicken Run Fell Race — a brilliant 14km fell race that climbs up and over the moorland above the village, with 433m of elevation gain and the option to "chicken out" at the shorter route (a choice none of our lot took!). The race is run in aid of Hayfield Primary School, finishing with homemade cakes and refreshments — as fell races go, it doesn't get much better. Well done to Lucie Tighe, Julie Smith, Mark Taylor, Paul Stevens and Christopher Mihalyi — a fantastic group effort! 👏

Sunday 15 March

Trotters 5 Mile

Paul Stevens — clearly not done after Saturday's fell race — was back out on Sunday along with Paul Cox and Craig Platt for the Trotters 5 Mile at Smithills, Bolton. Organised by Burnden Road Runners, it's a popular local community trail run on undulating woodland paths and trails — the kind of race that reminds you why you fell in love with running in the first place. Great efforts from all 3 of them! 👏

South Cheshire Harriers Secret 20

After Saturday's parkrun in Barnsley, Kath Biddle was straight back out on Sunday for the South Cheshire Harriers Secret 20 — 20 miles of quiet rural Cheshire lanes, finishing at the famous Hack Green Secret Nuclear Bunker near Nantwich. With only 280ft of climb, it's perfectly designed as spring marathon preparation, and Kath made it look anything but secret. Another fantastic day out! 👏

Bath Half Marathon

Andrew Whitehead completed a proper Bath double this weekend. Having run the Brickfields Park parkrun on Saturday morning, he was back out on Sunday to take on the Bath Half Marathon — one of the UK's most beloved half marathons, run every year since 1982 through the stunning streets of this historic city. The flat, two-lap course through the heart of Bath draws over 12,000 runners and is widely regarded as one of the fastest half marathon courses around. What a weekend away for Andrew! 👏

Trimpell 20

A massive well done to Paula Crossland, who completed the Trimpell 20 at Lancaster on Sunday — not only a brilliant race, but her very first 20-miler! Her son Harry ran it with her too, which makes it all the more special. The Trimpell follows the River Lune through the Lune Valley countryside on a flat, 90% traffic-free course that's ideal for spring marathon preparation. Paula absolutely nailed it, and reaching that 20-mile mark for the first time is something you never forget. Incredible achievement to both of them! 💜

Mellor 10k Trail Run

Rachel Bailey and Andy Gorman tackled the Mellor 10k Trail Run on Sunday — a stunning local trail race on the border of Stockport and the High Peak. The course follows the River Goyt valley before climbing past Brookbottom and up over Mellor Moor, taking in sweeping views toward Kinder Scout and the Manchester skyline, before a descent through Knowle Woods. Around 300m of climb and entirely off-road — a proper challenge. Brilliant efforts from both of them! 👏

🥾 Social Trail Run

Kingfisher Trail — Bromley Cross to Prestwich

Saturday also saw a brilliant group of 11 out on the Kingfisher Trail, running from Bromley Cross to Prestwich. Delphine, Nikki, Kate, Clive, Neil, Ken, Jane, Paul, Susanna, Jo and Debbie all made it out for another fantastic stretch of this wonderful long-distance route that runs all the way through to Prestwich.

Fell races, 20-milers, trail runs — this group really does it all! 💜

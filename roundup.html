Weekend Roundup

21 March 2026

🏃 Parkrun

What a glorious Saturday to be out running! With the sun shining and spring well and truly in the air, 12 RTR members headed to 11 different parkruns this week.

Bolton

Josh Young headed to Bolton and ran strongly, finishing 34th out of 309 runners on a gorgeous spring morning. A big shout out to Fiona and Derek Case who were on volunteering duty at Bolton this week - parkrun doesn't happen without brilliant volunteers like these two 👏

Chadderton Hall

Bernie Boardman flew the RTR flag at Chadderton Hall, putting in a solid effort among 143 runners on a lovely morning for it.

Fulwell Quarry

Stephen Crowe made the trip up to Fulwell Quarry near Sunderland, an undulating multi-terrain trail around a reclaimed nature reserve with a mix of grass and permanent paths. A great adventure on a beautiful day.

Huddersfield

Angela Pradena took on Huddersfield parkrun, joining 732 runners at Greenhead Park in West Yorkshire for a fine Saturday morning outing.

Morecambe Prom

Paul Cox made the most of the stunning weather with a trip to Morecambe Prom, a flat seafront run along the pedestrianised promenade with sweeping views of the Lake District mountains across the bay. A gorgeous setting for a Saturday morning run among 563 parkrunners.

Oldham

Dale Richards had a great morning at Oldham, running well among a field of 276.

Pennington Flash

Andrew and Alison Whitehead made it a family outing to Pennington Flash. Andrew led the way and picked up a course PB in the process - fantastic running! 💜 Alison enjoyed a parkwalk in the sunshine among the 125-strong field at this Wigan venue.

Sale Water

Martyn James enjoyed a parkwalk at Sale Water, soaking up the sunshine by the water among 248 parkrunners. A lovely way to spend a Saturday morning.

Woodbank

Paige Ashworth headed to Woodbank in Stockport, putting in a great run among 278 parkrunners on a beautiful spring morning.

Worsley Woods

Ros Dines tackled the trails at Worsley Woods, always a favourite Greater Manchester venue, running among a big field of 586.

Y Promenâd, Llandrillo-yn-Rhos

Kath Biddle headed to the North Wales coast for Y Promenâd parkrun, a scenic seaside route along the promenade between Combermere Gardens and Porth Eirias. A beautiful morning for a first visit to this lovely venue, running well among 387 parkrunners.

🏅 Races

Saturday 21 March

Sunnywood Village Link Challenge

Five RTR members took on the Sunnywood Village Link Challenge, a 20-mile endurance event starting from Burrs Country Park in Bury. Paula Crossland, Neil Naisbitt, Charlene Leyden, Lucie Tighe and Mark Taylor all tackled this local challenge on a perfect day for distance running. A great effort from everyone to take on 20 tough miles 👏

Runalot Backyard Ultra

Mike Aiken took on the Runalot Backyard Ultra in Ainsworth - a brutally simple format where runners complete a 6.7km loop every hour, on the hour, until they can't go any further. Mike showed incredible grit and determination, clocking up over 40 miles before calling it a day. That's a seriously impressive effort in a format that tests mental strength just as much as physical endurance 💜
